Under the hood

10 risk categories, checked in seconds

The same checks you take overtime to do manually — now automated.

01

Duplicate transactions

Same amount and description appearing within a 7-day window — a common indicator of accidental double-posting or deliberate manipulation.

02

Round number patterns

Exact multiples of RM500, RM1,000, RM5,000 or RM10,000 — particularly large debits, which rarely occur naturally in clean business accounts.

03

Structuring indicators

Transactions clustered just below RM10,000 may indicate deliberate layering to stay below detection or reporting thresholds.

04

Weekend activity

Transactions on Saturdays or Sundays are unusual for Malaysian SME businesses and often warrant a closer look.

05

Rapid sequential transfers

Three or more transfers to the same counterparty within a 48-hour window — a pattern associated with fund layering.

06

Statistical outliers

Any single transaction more than 3× the median value for the account — an amount well outside the normal operating range.

07

Vague high-value entries

Transactions above RM5,000 described only as CASH, TRANSFER, MISC or SUNDRY, with no counterparty or reference detail.

08

Balance discrepancies

Where the running balance between rows does not reconcile with the debit or credit shown — a potential sign of altered records.

09

Dormancy spikes

Seven or more days with no activity followed by sudden high-value transactions — accounts activated for specific, targeted purposes.

10

Reference sequence gaps

Where cheque or reference numbers suggest a sequence, missing numbers in that sequence may indicate destroyed or altered instruments.
